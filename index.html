Poor sleep + stress eating are the hidden #1 cause of belly fat. Cortisol (the stress hormone) directly signals your body to store fat around the belly. Fixing sleep will visibly accelerate your results.
Sleep protocol — target 7 hrs (your schedule)
Your window: 1:00 AM → 8:00 AM = 7 hours — workable, but tight for muscle recovery. Try to be in bed by 12:30–12:45 AM to get closer to 7.5 hrs when possible.
Wind-down starts at 12:30 AM — no more screens, no more food, no more work after this
Room temperature ~22–24°C and dark — even with a late bedtime, sleep quality determines recovery more than duration
No caffeine after 7:00 PM — caffeine has a 6-hour half-life, so anything after 7 PM directly delays your 1 AM sleep
1 glass warm turmeric milk at 12:30 AM — the casein in milk feeds muscle repair through the night
Beating stress eating
Never sit hungry at your desk — keep roasted chana, almonds, or fruit at your desk always
The 10-minute rule — when a craving hits, drink water and wait 10 min. 70% of cravings pass
Don't stock junk at home — if it isn't there, you can't eat it at 12 AM
Eat every 3–4 hours — the meal plan prevents hunger spikes that cause stress eating
Box breathing — when stressed: 4 sec in, 4 hold, 4 out. Lowers cortisol fast

Body soreness as a beginner is completely normal — your muscles are adapting to new stress. But tightness and recurring pain from your desk job + new gym routine need active management. This mobility plan runs alongside your workouts, not instead of them.
Know your pain — beginner's guide
✅ Normal soreness — keep going
  • Dull ache that starts 24–48 hrs after workout
  • Muscle feels tender when pressed
  • Stiffness that eases once you warm up
  • Soreness fades within 2–3 days
  • Both sides of body feel the same
🛑 Stop & rest — don't push through
  • Sharp or stabbing pain during exercise
  • Pain in joints (knees, shoulders, lower back)
  • Swelling, redness, or warmth around a joint
  • Pain only on one side of the body
  • Pain that gets worse as workout continues
When to do each routine
Before gym
Dynamic warm-up — 8–10 min. Moves your joints through full range. Never stretch cold muscles statically before lifting.
After gym
Static cool-down — 10–12 min. Hold each stretch. This is when flexibility is actually built.
Rest days
Full body flow — 15 min. Speeds recovery, reduces next-day soreness, improves posture from desk work.
Pain days
Gentle relief routine — skip the gym, do the pain-relief section below instead. Don't skip moving entirely.

Bonus: foam rolling (if available at gym)

Do this before your dynamic warm-up — 5 min

Quads — 30–40 sec each leg. Slow rolls, pause on tight spots. Reduces DOMS from squats significantly.
Upper back (thoracic) — Roll from mid-back to shoulders. Counteracts desk posture and tightness from bench press.
Glutes — Sit on roller, cross one ankle over opposite knee, roll. Releases the deep glute tension that causes lower back pain.
Calves — Stack one leg on the other for more pressure. 30 sec each. Prevents shin splints from HIIT sessions.
Rule: Roll slowly (1 inch/sec). If you hit a painful spot, stop and hold 20–30 sec — don't roll through it. Never roll directly on joints or the lower spine.
